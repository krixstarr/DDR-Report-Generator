▾
Primary Cause — Failed Waterproofing in Overhead Bathrooms: The bathrooms of Flat No. 203 (directly above) have open tile joints, degraded Nahani trap seals, and loose plumbing connections. Water from regular bathroom use percolates through the tile bed and floor slab into Flat No. 103.
Secondary Cause — Concealed Plumbing Leakage: Both inspection and thermal data confirm concealed plumbing leakage (marked "Yes" in checklist). Water from hidden pipe joints within walls and slabs is seeping through masonry causing the widespread skirting-level dampness.
Tertiary Cause — External Wall Cracks: Moderate cracks observed on external building facade near Master Bedroom area. These cracks, combined with corroded duct pipes, allow rainwater ingress directly into wall cavities — contributing to Master Bedroom wall dampness and efflorescence.
Contributing Factor — Brick-bat Coba Damage: Damage in Nahani trap and Brickbat Coba (waterproofing layer beneath tile flooring) in bathroom — confirmed in checklist ("Yes"). This layer, once compromised, allows water from the wet area to travel laterally into adjacent dry areas.
Thermal Evidence: Cold spots consistently recorded 3–8°C below surrounding surface temperatures at wall-floor junctions across all 30 scan points. This temperature differential is characteristic of moisture-saturated building materials causing evaporative cooling — a definitive indicator of active water presence within the structure.
